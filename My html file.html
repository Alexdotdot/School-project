Industrial revolution project

By Alexander Miller


The telegraph

The telegraph allowed for faster rate of communication via electrical signals. The telegraph was the beginning of modern day communications.

Invented by Samuel Morse



The Cotton Gin

The cotton Gin is a revolutionary tool used to separate cotton from its stem. It promoted slavery much do to the fact that less slaves were required to separate the cotton and more slaves were available to grow cotton.

Invented by Eli Whitney



The Lowell System

The Lowel System was a method of manufacturing where workers were provided with benefits Such as education. This system gave many women jobs, and led to worker's strikes, women's rights protests, and more education of women.

Invented by Francis Cabot Lowell




The Factory System

The Factory System was the idea of manufacturing being accomplished with machinery and labor division. This system largely increased cotton products production in the north, and required more cotton from the south.

Invented by Richard Arkwright

Interchangeable parts

The idea of interchangeable parts revolutionized the engineering field and created more efficient methods repair. This meant companies can hire a larger workforce. A more large work force increased product output as well as more jobs for the population. This idea also contributed an advantage for America during the war of 1812 against the British.

Invented by Eli Whitney

The Steam Engine

The Steam Engine used the energy from hot Steam to push a rotary object that then provided torque to its designated propellant. This engine was significant because it provided the foundation for many other inventions such as the steam locomotive.

Invented by Thomas Savery

The Steam Boat

The Steam Boat used a mechanism called the Steam Engine to bring torque to one or more fans that will turn and provide thrust on water. This invention was significant because it allowed boats to travel upstream.

Invented by Robert Fulton

The Bessemer Process

The Bessemer Process was a method of creating steel from a material called pig Iron. This provided a low cost method of converting iron to steel. The Bessemer Process was important because it shifted America's metal from iron to steel.

Invented by Sir Henry Bessemer

The Steam Locomotive

The Steam Locomotive was a train powered by the Steam engine. This invention promoted long-distance travel across the country, as well as increased manufacturing production. This made habitation of unpopulated lands more possible, which increased the economy.

Invented by George Stephenson
